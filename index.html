Biography

Shubham is a Ph.D. student in transportation engineering at Purdue University with particular interests in cognitive science, information-based feedback, alternative fuel transportation, and connected and autonomous technologies. During his master’s studies at Purdue University, Shubham developed a framework to analyze the impacts of electric vehicles on traffic network performance, and illustrated that with proper planning and regulations, electric vehicles can not only reduce emissions, but can also mitigate traffic congestion. He received his bachelor’s degree from Indian Institute of Technology Bombay in 2012. Currently, Shubham lives in West Lafayette, Indiana.

Selected Publications

Routing aspects of electric vehicle drivers and their effects on network performance

This study formultes a multi-class dynamic user equilibrium model to determine the equilibrium flows for mixed traffic consisting of BEVs and ICEVs. The results illustrate that the differences in route choice behavior of BEV and ICEV drivers can synergistically lead to reduction in total travel time and the network performance towards system optimum under certain conditions.
Shubham Agrawal, Hong Zheng, Srinivas Peeta, Amit Kumar
